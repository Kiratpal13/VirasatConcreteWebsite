How Much Does a Concrete Driveway Cost in Calgary?

Published: April 2, 2026 | 7 min read

Planning a new driveway in Calgary? Cost is likely your first question. Concrete driveway pricing depends on several factors—size, base preparation, finish, and local market conditions. This guide breaks down what you'll pay, why prices vary so much, and how to get the best value for your investment.

Concrete Driveway Cost Breakdown for Calgary (2026)

Standard Concrete Driveway (Broom Finish)

A typical Calgary driveway is 30 feet long × 20 feet wide = 600 square feet.

Calgary 2026 Pricing Range: $4,200-$7,200 ($7-$12 per square foot)

What this includes: Excavation, gravel base, 4-inch concrete slab, broom finish, standard drainage

Regional variation: SE/SW Calgary (longer distance from concrete suppliers) costs 5-10% more than downtown/NW areas

Stamped or Decorative Concrete

Calgary 2026 Pricing Range: $7,500-$14,000 ($12.50-$23 per square foot)

What adds cost: Custom pattern stamps, color pigments, sealant, design complexity

Textured or Exposed Aggregate

Calgary 2026 Pricing Range: $5,400-$9,600 ($9-$16 per square foot)

What adds cost: Special finishing, sealing, decorative aggregate exposure

The 10 Biggest Factors Affecting Your Driveway Cost

1. Driveway Size (Square Footage)

This is the primary cost driver. Larger driveways have lower per-square-foot costs due to economies of scale.

Driveway Size Approx. Cost Cost Per Sq Ft
Single car (250 sq ft) $1,750-$3,000 $7-$12
Double car (400 sq ft) $2,800-$4,800 $7-$12
Standard double (600 sq ft) $4,200-$7,200 $7-$12
Large family (1,000 sq ft) $7,000-$12,000 $7-$12

2. Site Preparation (Excavation & Base)

This is where hidden costs often appear.

  • Good site (minimal excavation, level ground): $500-$1,000 (included in standard quotes)
  • Poor drainage (requires grading adjustment): Add $400-$800
  • Sloped property (more excavation required): Add $600-$1,200
  • Rocky soil (rock removal): Add $800-$2,000
  • Existing asphalt removal: Add $1-2 per square foot ($250-$1,200)
  • Soft/wet soil (requires deeper base or fill): Add $1,000-$3,000

Calgary-specific note: Calgary's clay soil is usually stable, so base prep is typically moderate cost. However, properties near wetlands or with poor drainage may cost more.

3. Concrete Thickness

Standard residential driveways are 4 inches thick. Heavier loads require more.

  • 4 inches (standard): Base price
  • 5 inches (reinforced): +$0.50-$1 per sq ft
  • 6 inches (heavy-duty, RVs): +$1-$2 per sq ft
  • With reinforcement mesh/rebar: +$0.75-$1.50 per sq ft

4. Finish Type

Broom finish (standard): Base price—provides traction and is maintenance-free

Smooth trowel finish: +$0.50-$1 per sq ft—looks modern but requires sealing

Stamped/Decorative pattern: +$5-$11 per sq ft—significant upgrade for custom designs

Exposed aggregate: +$2-$6 per sq ft—elegant look, requires sealing

Colored concrete: +$1-$3 per sq ft—adds personality and hides stains

5. Sealing (Initial or Annual)

Most quotes DON'T include sealing. Plan for additional costs:

  • Initial seal coat (first year): $300-$600 for standard driveway
  • Premium sealers (polyurethane, siliconate): $400-$800
  • Annual maintenance sealing (years 2+): $250-$400

6. Drainage & Sloping

Proper drainage is critical in Calgary to prevent water pooling and freeze damage.

  • Standard slope (included): 1/8 inch per foot drop (part of base quote)
  • Enhanced drainage (swales, edge drains): +$500-$1,500
  • Catchbasin installation: +$400-$800 each

7. Access & Site Difficulty

  • Easy access (straight, flat): Standard price
  • Tight access (narrow street, gates): +$500-$1,000 (pump truck costs more)
  • Sloped or complex shape: +$1,000-$3,000
  • Residential street (permits/traffic control): +$200-$500

8. Time of Year (Seasonal Variation)

Calgary's short construction season affects pricing:

  • Peak season (May-August): Highest prices, longer waits (4-8 weeks)
  • Shoulder season (April, September): Moderate prices, 2-4 week waits
  • Fall/Winter (October-March): Lower prices but concrete cure time slower; many contractors unavailable

9. Concrete Mix Quality

  • Standard mix: Base price (3,000-3,500 PSI)
  • Air-entrained (freeze-thaw resistant): +$0.25-$0.50 per sq ft (RECOMMENDED for Calgary)
  • High-performance mix: +$0.50-$1 per sq ft

Calgary note: Air-entrained mix is essential in Calgary due to freeze-thaw cycles. Don't skimp on this.

10. Additional Features

  • Control joints/saw cutting: Included in standard
  • Expansion joints: +$0.25 per linear foot
  • Integrated heating (snow melt): +$3,000-$6,000 total
  • Apron/transition edges: +$200-$600
  • Permeable concrete: +$3-$5 per sq ft

Questions to Ask Contractors for Accurate Quotes

When getting quotes from Calgary concrete contractors, ask these questions to ensure accurate pricing:

  • Is site preparation included, or is it extra? (Clarify scope)
  • Is sealing included, or is it quoted separately?
  • What concrete mix are you using? (Ensure air-entrained for freeze-thaw resistance)
  • What is the warranty on your work? (Standard: 1-2 years)
  • Are permits and inspections included in the quote?
  • When can you start, and what's the guaranteed finish date?
  • Do you handle drainage and slope to code?
  • What happens if weather delays the project?
  • Is this a fixed-price quote or an estimate?

Ways to Save Money on Your Driveway (Without Sacrificing Quality)

1. Choose Broom Finish Instead of Decorative

Broom finish is attractive, functional, and costs $4-8 per sq ft vs. $12-23 for stamped. You can always add decorative features later.

2. Schedule in Shoulder Season (April or September)

Spring and fall pricing is 10-20% lower than summer peak season. If you can wait until September (also ideal for fall sealing), you'll save $500-$1,500.

3. Prepare the Site Yourself

If you have the equipment and labor, removing existing asphalt or grading can save $500-$1,500. But hire pros for the concrete itself.

4. Seal DIY (if Budget is Tight)

Acrylic sealing is DIY-friendly ($100-200 materials) but requires annual reapplication. Professional sealing costs more upfront but lasts 3-10 years.

5. Get Multiple Quotes

Don't accept the first quote. Get 3-4 quotes from established Calgary contractors. Prices vary by $1,000-$3,000 for the same scope.

6. Bundle Services

Getting a patio or garage pad done at the same time often reduces per-square-foot costs (saves contractor setup/mobilization fees).

Calgary Homeowner Pricing Questions

Q: Why do driveway quotes vary so much ($3,000 vs. $8,000 for same size)?

Significant differences usually indicate different scope: site prep costs, concrete quality, finish type, sealing, and contractor overhead/profit margins. Always compare apples-to-apples (same concrete mix, same prep scope, same finish).

Q: Is a concrete driveway worth it vs. asphalt?

Concrete: Higher upfront cost ($6-12/sq ft) but lasts 30+ years, lower maintenance. Total 30-year cost: ~$1,000-2,000 (mostly sealing).

Asphalt: Lower upfront cost ($3-6/sq ft) but lasts 15-20 years, requires resurfacing ($2-4/sq ft) every 5-7 years. Total 30-year cost: ~$8,000-15,000.

Winner: Concrete for Calgary (freeze-thaw is hard on asphalt).

Q: When should I seal my new driveway?

Wait 30-60 days after concrete cures (typically 90 days in Calgary's cool climate). Then seal in late spring or early September.

Q: How long do concrete driveways last in Calgary?

30-40 years with proper maintenance (annual or biennial sealing). Neglected driveways last 15-20 years before major repairs.
